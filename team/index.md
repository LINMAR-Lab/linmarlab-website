The laboratory is led by **Raúl Montañez** and **Ignacio J. Melero-Jiménez**, whose work brings together complementary perspectives from **theoretical and computational biology** and **experimental microbial evolution**.

Raúl Montañez works at the intersection of **complex systems, network science, and theoretical biology**, developing mathematical and computational approaches to understand how biological systems are organised and how they change over time. His research explores topics such as ecological networks, host–pathogen interactions, and the emergence of structure and complexity in living systems.

Ignacio J. Melero-Jiménez focuses on **experimental evolution and microbial ecology**, studying how microbial populations and communities adapt to environmental change. His work combines laboratory evolution experiments with genomic and ecological analyses to investigate processes such as **evolutionary rescue, microbial interactions, and adaptation under environmental stress**.

Together, the lab integrates **experimental and theoretical approaches** to understand how ecological interactions, metabolic dependencies, and environmental change shape evolutionary dynamics.
