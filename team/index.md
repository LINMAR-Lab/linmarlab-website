The laboratory is led by **Raúl Montañez** and **Ignacio J. Melero-Jiménez**, whose research brings together complementary expertise in **theoretical and computational biology** and **experimental microbial evolution**.

Raúl Montañez works at the interface between **complex systems, network science, and theoretical biology**, developing mathematical and computational approaches to understand the structure and dynamics of biological systems. His work has explored topics such as ecological networks, host–pathogen interactions, and the emergence of complex organisation in biological systems.

Ignacio J. Melero-Jiménez focuses on **experimental evolution and microbial ecology**, investigating how microbial populations and communities adapt to environmental change. His research integrates laboratory evolution experiments with genomic analyses and ecological theory to understand processes such as **evolutionary rescue, microbial interactions, and adaptation under environmental stress**.

Together, the lab combines **experimental and theoretical approaches** to study how ecological interactions, metabolic dependencies, and environmental change shape the evolution and stability of microbial systems.
