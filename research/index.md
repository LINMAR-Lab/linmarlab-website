# {% include icon.html icon="fa-solid fa-microscope" %} Research

Understanding how biological systems evolve, interact, and adapt to changing environments.

Our research integrates **experimental approaches, microbial ecology, and theoretical and computational modelling** to investigate the mechanisms that shape **adaptation, ecological interactions, and evolutionary dynamics**. By combining laboratory experiments with quantitative models, we aim to understand how biological systems respond to environmental stress and how interactions between organisms influence evolutionary outcomes.

{% include section.html %}

## Research Areas

### Evolutionary dynamics

We investigate how populations adapt to environmental stress and how evolutionary processes influence the persistence and stability of biological systems. Our work explores processes such as **adaptation, evolutionary rescue, and eco-evolutionary feedbacks** using experimental and theoretical approaches.

### Microbial interactions

Biological systems are shaped by interactions between organisms. We study how **cooperation, competition, and metabolic dependencies** structure communities and influence evolutionary trajectories, from simple microbial systems to complex ecological networks.

### Mathematical and computational modelling

To complement experimental work, we develop **theoretical and computational models** that allow us to understand and predict the dynamics of biological systems. These include models of **eco-evolutionary dynamics, interaction networks, and constraint-based metabolic modelling such as flux balance analysis (FBA).**
